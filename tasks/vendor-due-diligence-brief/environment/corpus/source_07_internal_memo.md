# Internal Due Diligence Memo — CloudEdge Systems

*Prepared by: Procurement & Legal Review Team*
*Classification: Internal — Do Not Distribute*
*Date: April 2026*

## Summary

This memo documents preliminary findings from our internal review of CloudEdge Systems ahead of tomorrow's partnership discussion.

## Open Legal Matter

Our legal team identified an active IP dispute with ForgeNet Inc. (filed Q3 2024, case docket DE-2024-CV-7821). ForgeNet alleges that CloudEdge's DataGuard encryption key rotation mechanism infringes on ForgeNet's US Patent No. 11,234,567. CloudEdge's legal team has filed a counter-motion, but the case has not yet been resolved. If the court rules against CloudEdge, they may be required to re-engineer a core component of DataGuard or pay licensing royalties, potentially impacting product stability and roadmap.

## Financial Observations

- Company was founded in 2022 and has only 3 years of operating history.
- Series A funding ($18M) was raised in 2023; no Series B has been announced.
- Runway estimate suggests a fundraising event will be needed by late 2026 or early 2027.
- Customer base of 14 enterprises is relatively small for a vendor asking for a 12-month minimum commitment.

## Recommendation from Legal

Do not sign any contract referencing DataGuard's key rotation module until the ForgeNet dispute is resolved or CloudEdge provides written indemnification covering IP infringement claims. Flag for executive review before proceeding.
