# Global Regulation of Genetically Modified Organisms (GMOs)

## Introduction

This document reviews regulatory frameworks for genetically modified organisms in agriculture and food production. It is not directly related to CRISPR therapeutic applications.

## Regional Approaches

### United States
The FDA, USDA, and EPA share oversight depending on the product type. Gene-edited crops may face less stringent review than transgenic organisms.

### European Union
The EU Court of Justice ruled in 2018 that gene-edited organisms fall under existing GMO legislation, requiring strict authorization.

### China
China has developed a tiered regulatory system for gene-edited agricultural products, with some categories eligible for simplified review.

## Key Regulatory Themes
1. Product-based vs. process-based regulation
2. Labeling requirements
3. Environmental risk assessment
4. Consumer acceptance

## Conclusion

GMO regulation varies significantly by jurisdiction. This document is provided as general background on biotechnology governance.
