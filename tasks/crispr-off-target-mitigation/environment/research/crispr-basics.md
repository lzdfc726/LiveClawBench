# CRISPR-Cas9 Basic Principles

## Introduction

CRISPR (Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats) is a revolutionary gene-editing technology derived from bacterial immune systems.

## Core Mechanism

### 1. guide RNA (gRNA)
A synthetic RNA molecule that guides the Cas9 nuclease to a specific DNA sequence through complementary base pairing.

### 2. Cas9 Nuclease
An enzyme that creates double-strand breaks (DSBs) at the target site specified by the gRNA.

### 3. DNA Repair Pathways
- **NHEJ (Non-Homologous End Joining)**: Error-prone, introduces insertions/deletions
- **HDR (Homology-Directed Repair)**: Precise, requires a repair template

## Key Applications
- Gene knockout
- Gene insertion
- Base editing
- Prime editing

## Off-Target Effects
Cas9 can cleave at unintended genomic sites with similar sequences to the target. This is a major safety concern for therapeutic applications.

## Note

This document provides general CRISPR background. Off-target mitigation strategies require dedicated investigation.
