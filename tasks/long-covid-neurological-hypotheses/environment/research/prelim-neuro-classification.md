# Preliminary Classification of Long COVID Neurological Symptoms

## Overview of Neurological Manifestations

Based on early clinical observations (2020-2022), Long COVID neurological symptoms can be broadly grouped into the following categories:

### 1. Cognitive Symptoms
- Memory impairment
- Difficulty concentrating
- Word-finding difficulties
- Executive function deficits

### 2. Sensory Symptoms
- Loss or alteration of taste/smell (though this often resolves within months)
- Visual disturbances
- Tinnitus

### 3. Motor Symptoms
- Muscle weakness
- Tremor (rare)
- Coordination difficulties

### 4. Autonomic Symptoms
- Orthostatic intolerance
- Temperature dysregulation
- Heart rate variability issues

## Preliminary Hypotheses (Early-Stage)

Some researchers have suggested possible mechanisms:
- Direct viral neuroinvasion (though evidence is limited)
- Immune-mediated damage
- Microvascular dysfunction
- Mitochondrial dysfunction

## Limitations of This Survey

This classification is based primarily on patient self-reports and small observational studies. Large-scale, controlled studies with biomarker validation are still needed to confirm these categories and understand underlying mechanisms.
