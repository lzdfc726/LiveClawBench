# Long COVID Background Overview

## What is Long COVID?

Long COVID, also known as post-acute sequelae of SARS-CoV-2 infection (PASC), refers to a range of symptoms that continue for weeks or months after the acute phase of a COVID-19 infection has resolved. The WHO defines Long COVID as occurring in individuals with a history of probable or confirmed SARS-CoV-2 infection, usually 3 months from the onset of COVID-19, with symptoms that last for at least 2 months and cannot be explained by an alternative diagnosis.

## Common Symptoms

The most frequently reported symptoms include fatigue, shortness of breath, cognitive dysfunction ("brain fog"), muscle weakness, joint pain, and sleep disturbances.

## Prevalence Estimates

Studies suggest that approximately 10-30% of COVID-19 survivors experience some form of Long COVID. However, prevalence estimates vary widely depending on the study design, population, and definition used.

## Note on Scope

This document provides general background. Specific neurological mechanisms and treatment approaches require further dedicated investigation.
