# Preliminary Investigation: USPTO and Copyright Office Positions on AI-Generated Works

## US Copyright Office Guidance (March 2023)

The U.S. Copyright Office issued guidance stating that:
- Works created solely by AI without human involvement are not copyrightable
- Works with sufficient human creative input may be copyrightable
- Applicants must disclose AI involvement

## Key Cases (Preliminary Survey)

### Thaler v. Perlmutter (2023)
- Plaintiff: Stephen Thaler (AI system "DABUS")
- Issue: Can an AI be listed as an inventor/author?
- Outcome: Court held that human authorship is required under the Copyright Act

### Zarya of the Dawn (2023)
- Comic book with images generated by Midjourney
- Copyright Office: Granted copyright for the text and arrangement, but not for the AI-generated images
- Significance: Human creative selection and arrangement may be protectable even when individual elements are AI-generated

## Limitations of This Survey

This is a preliminary overview focused on U.S. developments only. A comprehensive analysis would need to cover EU jurisprudence, UK sui generis provisions for computer-generated works, Chinese PIPL and copyright law interactions, Japanese and Korean approaches, and ongoing legislative developments globally.
