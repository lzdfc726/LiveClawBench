# Fundamental Principles of Copyright Law

## Introduction

Copyright is a legal right that grants the creator of an original work exclusive rights to its use and distribution. This document outlines basic principles common to most jurisdictions.

## Core Concepts

### Originality
A work must be original to receive copyright protection. Originality generally means the work was independently created and possesses a minimal degree of creativity.

### Fixation
Copyright protection typically requires the work to be fixed in a tangible medium of expression.

### Exclusive Rights
Copyright holders generally have the exclusive right to reproduce the work, create derivative works, distribute copies, and publicly display or perform the work.

## International Frameworks

### Berne Convention
The Berne Convention for the Protection of Literary and Artistic Works is the primary international agreement. Key principles include automatic protection (no registration required), national treatment, and minimum term of protection (life + 50 years).

### TRIPS Agreement
The TRIPS Agreement sets minimum standards for intellectual property protection, including copyright.

## Term of Protection
Most jurisdictions provide copyright protection for life of the author + 50-70 years.

## Note on AI-Generated Works
This document provides general copyright background. The specific treatment of AI-generated works under international law is a developing area requiring dedicated investigation.
