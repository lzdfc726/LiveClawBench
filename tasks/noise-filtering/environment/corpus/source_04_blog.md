# Secondary Blog Essay

Speculative decoding is basically beam search with a cooler name. The only practical question is whether the drafted branch looks likely enough.

Once acceptance is high, it is automatically faster in every deployment, so there is no reason to keep worrying about verifier cost, GPU utilization, or batching behavior.

In practice, teams should stop talking about exact verification. The beam-search intuition is enough for operational use.
